PROVENLY — WHITEPAPER
Proof-of-process portfolios for the AI era
July 2026

THE PROBLEM

Hiring and freelancing run on portfolios and work samples — and AI has made both unfalsifiable. Harvard Business Review's June 2026 assessment is blunt: "AI has broken hiring." A Robert Half survey (March 2026) finds 67% of HR leaders report AI-generated applications are actively slowing their hiring; Forbes reports the same share of managers saying AI résumés are sabotaging the process. Every opening now attracts thousands of near-identical, polished, generated submissions.

The people hurt most are the genuine makers: designers, writers, developers and researchers whose finished work is now indistinguishable — at a glance — from a prompt's output. The finished artifact no longer proves anything. Peer-reviewed research adds a twist: labeling work "AI-free" is unverifiable, and blanket AI disclosures actually erode trust (the "transparency dilemma", OBHDP 2025).

What can't be faked retroactively is process: the brief, the dead ends, the revisions, the client pushback, accumulated over days and weeks with timestamps that already existed before anyone asked for proof.

THE SOLUTION

Provenly is the simplest possible tool for making process portable and verifiable:

1. Start a piece — a proof timeline for any project, created the day work begins.
2. Log stages as they happen — brief received, sketches, drafts, revisions, delivery. Each entry is timestamped at logging time and sealed into a SHA-256 hash chain: every stage's hash incorporates the previous stage's hash, so a history fabricated after the fact breaks the chain visibly.
3. Share one link — a public proof page showing the journey. Attach it to job applications, freelance proposals, Dribbble shots, GitHub READMEs.

Provenly does not claim to detect AI, and doesn't need to. It shifts the burden of proof: a candidate with a six-week, twelve-stage documented history is categorically more credible than a portfolio that appeared fully formed. Honest AI-assisted workflows can be documented too — the timeline shows how the tools were used, which is exactly what employers say they want to know (Forbes, 2026: employers use AI but don't trust candidates who hide it).

WHY NOW

- The generative flood hit hiring hardest in 2025–26; screening teams are actively searching for signals that survive AI.
- Creators need differentiation this quarter — job seekers in the 2026 layoff market, freelancers competing against $5 AI gigs.
- No incumbent: AI detectors are unreliable and adversarial; portfolio hosts (Behance, Dribbble) showcase outcomes, not process; version control (Git) proves process only for code, only for the technical, and only if reviewers dig.

MARKET

B2C wedge: individual creators and job seekers — free for 3 pieces, $8/mo unlimited. 
B2B expansion: hiring teams (verify candidate proof pages, request proof-of-process as part of take-home tasks), freelance marketplaces (proof-backed listings), design schools (student portfolios with provenance from day one).
The wedge market is every knowledge-work job seeker and freelancer facing AI competition — tens of millions of people; Upwork's 2026 Future Workforce Index documents both the AI pressure and the migration of value toward provably human judgment.

BUSINESS MODEL

Freemium B2C ($8/mo), team plans for agencies ($29/mo, client-facing proof pages with branding), recruiter verification API later. Zero marginal cost per proof page; product-led growth — every shared proof page is an ad.
